ID Hatshop Logo Design

In this projects, I designed a logo for ID Hatshop. In phase one, I collected other logotypes designed for similar organizations. Then brainstormed and sketched and turn best 3 sketches into logotype using Adobe Illustrator. In phase two, I finalized my logotype(including typeface) from all options in phase one and completed the design. Besides, my desgin also included the typography guide, logotype scalability, mandatory free space guide, 6 variants of color conversions, the logo on the building facade and brand tag design.
See ID Hatshop Facebook.
Client
ID Hatshop
Skill
Graphic Design.
Tool
Adobe Illustrator.
Adobe Photoshop.

Concept

Overall Style: Minimalism.


As our client wants to have a logo of clean and modern style at the same time, I was trying to simplify the woman on their business card to be more like a logo, and added modern elements on it -- the Monotype Corsiva typeface.

Typeface: Monotype Corsiva.


I choose Monotype Corsiva as the font on logo, which is more complex compared to the logo itself. Because I think simple logo pairs with a little bit complex typeface can maintain the balance. And this typeface is like hand-written but more readable, as well as looks modern. The font-size of this signature is 90pt, which is readable but not being emphasized too much.

Color Palette


I wanted to design a logo which could be very styled and keep some of their original elements. I looked at their current business card, and thus I wanted to keep their dark blue, red colors and the woman on this card.


Design Process

Rough Logotype.


I choosed three from my sketches and created the rough logotype in Adobe Illustrator. I used Segoe Script for those logos, becasue script is styled and fashioned. Customer wants something simple but fasioned, that’s why I choose fancy typeface to accompany with simple logo. If both of them are simple, it’s gonna be dull.

Phase II.


After I received feedbacks, I found the elements of red lips and hat which covered eyes work really well. So I decided to keep them and refine those elements in a more elegant way. Based on this sketch(Figure 5), I created the final logo.
