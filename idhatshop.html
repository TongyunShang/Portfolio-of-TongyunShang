ID Hatshop Logo Design

In this projects, I designed a logo for ID Hatshop, a small hatseller who customizes hat for customers. In phase one, I collected other logotypes designed for similar organizations. Then brainstormed, sketched and turned my 3 best sketches into logotype using Adobe Illustrator. In phase two, I finalized my logotype (including typeface) from the 3 sketches in phase one and completed the design. My design also included the typography guide, logotype scalability, mandatory free space guide, 6 variants of color conversions, the logo on the building facade and brand tag design.
See ID Hatshop Facebook.
Client
ID Hatshop
Skill
Graphic Design.
Tool
Adobe Illustrator.
Adobe Photoshop.

Concept

Overall Style: Minimalism.


As our client wanted to have a clean and modern logo, I tried to simplify the woman on their business card to be more like a logo, and added modern elements on it -- the Monotype Corsiva typeface.

Typeface: Monotype Corsiva.


I chose Monotype Corsiva as the font on the logo, a more complex style compared to the rest pf the logo, because I thought a simple logo paired with complex typeface maintains an elequent balance. This typeface also looks hand-written but is more readable, as well as looking modern. The font-size of this signature is 90pt, which is readable but not overemphasized.

Color Palette


I wanted to design a logo which would be fashionable yet keep some of the original elements from their business card. Looking at their current business card, I loved the color pallete -a mix of dark blue and red colors - and the woman on the card, and then I chose to maintain these elements in a new design.

Since the customer wanted something simple but fasionable, I chose a fancy typeface to accompany the simple logo, each logo involved a simple design. If both were simple, it would be dull.
