UMSI Website Redesign

In this projects, I redesigned the website for University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI). In phase one, I reiewed the website of a peer institution and collected screenshots of 2 sites whose design I'd hope to see on the UMSI website. Besed on those content, I prepared two ideas for UMSI homepage redesign and stocked relevant images. In phase two, I repositioned, resized the informational blocks from current UMSI website and added elements consistent with current design trends including minimalism, free apace, hero image, etc.
See current UMSI website.
Client
University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI).
Skill
Graphic Design.
Tool
Adobe Illustrator.
Adobe Photoshop.

Concept

Modern Trends.


I include big hero images on the top of the page, video content and relevant images for each section. By utilizing other trends, I use minimal text, sliders, organized but not stubborn arrangements for various content, and liberal free space. Additionally, I encompass social media icons in three different places, one is in the “share” drop down menu in navigation, another one is in the “Social Media Feeds”, and the last one is in footer. Furthermore, calling for “GIVE” is also very necessary, so I put the GIVE button on the very top.

Color Palette.


The color palette I choose is from our UMichigan Website Homepage, which uses blue, yellow and gray. I also keep some similar elements from UMich homepage: transparent backgrounds, UMSI News rectangle elements, and Social Media Feeds layout. This is for keep consistency for evoking the feeling that UMSI is part of UMichigan but has our own characteristics.


Typeface: Century Gothic pair with Myriad pro.


Century Gothic looks neat and modern style as title which is consistent with UMSI's mission to engage people in useing technology to build a better world, while the Myriad pro is more readable for visitors.
