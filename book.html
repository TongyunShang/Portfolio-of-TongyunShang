BooksMap Mobile APP

In this project, I prototyped an application that enables students in UMich to find inexpensive used books, or sell idled books to others. There are three unique features of this APP: using map to locate nearby users and contact them; providing 2 options for users to post or search information via Have or Need; pricing/paying with a dinner/concert/movie, etc., which both buyers and sellers can accept or not.
See Paper Prototype.
Target Population
Students in Umichigan
Skill

Problem Statement

Students often find books through three methods: borrowing in libraries, buying in bookstores or purchasing online. However, books in libraries are often in shortage, those in bookstores might be expensive, and it takes time shipping if purchased online. What if students want to get books instantly and in proper cost? Meanwhile, there are some students who have trouble dealing with their idled books, which still look new but wouldn’t be used anymore. So how could those students meet with each other?

Solution Overview

What I am trying to do is providing those students an access to inexpensive books as fast as possible while others dealing with their idled books. I will create an application, where both sides of students can share their information of books they have/want, prices they feel comfortable with and locations they would like to exchange the books, etc. By doing so, they can reach the nearest users who have or need a book and would like to meet with them.
This application would only work when there are a lot of users, so I decide to firstly narrow down the community to just students on central campus, where larger amount of students stay. But my target community is not limited to students on central campus. To avoid the situation users cannot find books they want and then never come back to use this APP again, I give users alternative option to post their needs first. Additionally, in order to attract more users and make this platform interesting, I will add one feature where users can price/pay their books with a dinner/concert/movie, etc.

Design Process

Initial Design.


I want to build an application, where users from both sides could exchange their information directly. Users can post their second-hand books on this application with different tags, while others can use tags to search for books with distance shown next to each item. And then they can contact with any other user off-line.Additionally, my project also encompasses a function that users can rent out and borrow books at desk in libraries, where there is a bookshelf to put second-books and a librarian in charge of the whole operation. Information of those books will also be put in this application with distance as an option for users. This application also provides book information from libraries and bookstores as alternatives for comparisons. This idea was generated from one of my sketch in storyboard (Figure 1).


Figure 1. Sketch in 8 solutions’ storyboard

Critical Features.


  • •  Directly connect users who need books with those who can provide idled second-hand books

  • •  Provide information retrieving feature of “Second-hand Books Library”

  • •  Provide information of the same books from school libraries and bookstores

Reflection & Changes.


According to the reading the Design of Everyday Things by Donald A.Norman, well-designed products should be clearly used with lessinstructions or even without any text-based information. Just thevisual, acoustic information themselves, being consistent with as many as possible of the four constraints, are enough to guide users to utilize them properly. I can’t agree more on Donald’s standpoint, so consider it as my most important design philosophy.
However, it is hard to achieve. After I built my paper prototypes(Figure 2 & 3) and showed them to my peers, they felt really confusedabout each feature, even though I explained all the time. Good design should be introductions the products themselves, but I explained a lot because they were way too much complex.
