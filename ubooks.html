UbookS

The Motivation

Americans today have a variety of content available in various formats and through a range of digitally connected devices. The share of Americans that read the e-book is increasing, but print books remain much more popular than books in digital formats according to the Pew Research Center survey in 2016.

College students are part of this print book consuming group because of the textbook requirements. They recognize that textbooks are essential to their education but have been pushed to the breaking point by skyrocketing costs. They turn to the library for free copies but only find limited sources have already been checked out. They want to borrow or purchase used books from seniors but don't know who to ask. So they finally decide to buy from Amazon, and they are informed the shipping takes 7 days.

All these problems occurred through the process of acquiring a textbook necessitate a cheaper and faster access to connect the demand side and the supply side.

Design Process

Research

Right after I got the inspiration to solve the textbooks issue, I conducted 3 user testing as the pilot study and then identified the 3 pain points are price(how to lower the price), time(how to save time) and connection(how to connect the demand side and supply side).

The User

To specify the frustrations and needs, I performed 7 in-depth interviews by asking the following 5 questions:

  1. What are the frustrations you had met when you bought textbooks? Why you felt frustrated?
  2. What are the sources/accesses they often use to purchase the textbook? What do you like or hate them?
  3. What do you think about used books? If you are going to buy the used textbooks, what would be the aspects you consider?
  4. How do you often dispose of the your unused books? Why?
  5. How comfortable are you to meet with other students you don't know to buy their books? What would be the concern?

Competitive Analysis

I did 5 competitive analysis based on secondary research and the sources mentioned by prospective users. After analyzing their pros and cons, I extracted some useful aspects from them which provided some helpful insights and helped me to make design decisions.

Design Goal

Connect demands with supplies and help them find the best match in an efficient way.

Define

Based on the research insights gained from interviews and competitive analysis, I decided to pursue the idea of designing a mobile application through which both sides of students who want to buy and sell used textbooks can reach out to the right person at a satisfying price and in an efficient way.

1. On-campus platform: students can trade their books on campus which can be faster within walking distance and ensure safety as well.
2. Flexible book-trade method: the mobile app doesn't support online payment. Students can trade their books face-to-face which not only facilitate the connection but also more efficient.
3. Focus on used textbooks: used textbooks can be cheaper. And textbook-focused can reduce distractions and make the platform run more efficiently.
4. Purchase and sell: supporting both purchase and sell books can better match the supply side and demand side, reducing the waste of resources.

Ideate

Candidate Features

Developed from the insights gathered above, I distilled the primary goals into the design goal matrix depending on 2 separate personas (figure 1). I generated some possible solutions corresponded to each of the goals. For those problems I couldn't decide how to implement, I used Qustions, Options, and CriteriA(QOC) method (figure 2) to list out all the trade-offs, and refined the following features:

Search and browse feature: to find the target books or user faster, search paired with browse feature can work more efficiently. To narrow down the scope of searching, filters and sort features are needed as well. So, it's also necessary to have clear categories and labels assigned to textbooks.

Recommendation feature: to assist users in connecting with others, the system can pop-out the best match user which shortens the searching time. Similarly, the system can also recommend relevant textbooks based on view history.

Efficient creat-new-post process: a long and detailed post form will scare the user. A very brief form with only useful required fields is necessary. An intuitive and simple design is essential too.

Keep users updated: if the user saved a search, then he will receive the notification if there is any new post. The claimed textbooks or out-dated posts will be removed from the interfaces to cause less distraction.

Compare feature: users can compare the posted price with a lateral view of the original price of that book. If they can't make up their minds, they can always save to their favorites and compare different sources later.

Review feature: depending on how many successful deals a user have had and the ratings of reliability, the other users can better evaluate who should they spend time discussing the next step.

Scenarios

To explore how users would use the platform, I created 3 scenarios based on possible features generated above. The stories cover the three primary use cases of this mobile app for both seller and buyer personas.



Rapid Prototype

I evaluated the possible features and scenarios by making low fidelity prototypes and tested with 5 users. In the initial design, I created the main pages, including sign-in/sign-up, settings, homepage, search, saved, profile and inbox.

Insights

After testing the lo-fi prototypes with 5 users, I gained the following central insights correlated to the candidate features.

1. Search feature: listing existing posts when do searching can be very helpful.
2. Recommendation feature: the criteria need to be defined, and the percentage is not intuitive.
3. Creat-new-post process: instead of typing on the mobile phone, the user is more willing to choose and the less input, the better.
4. Saved-search feature: they like the idea of saved-search and keep them notified of a new post. They also wish to create alert of certain notification and option to turn it off.ion if there is any new post. The claimed textbooks or out-dated posts will be removed from the interfaces to cause less distraction.
5. Filters and tabs: the "sell" and "buy" tabs are confusing and they want to narrow down the search by choosing department or classes too.
