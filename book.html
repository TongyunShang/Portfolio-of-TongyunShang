BooksMap Mobile APP

In this project, I prototyped an application that enables students at the University of Michigan to find inexpensive used books, or sell unneeded books to others. This application ahs three unique features: using a map to locate nearby users and facilitate contact and know them; providing 2 options for users to post or search information via Have or Need ; paying with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.
See Paper Prototype.

Skill

Problem Statement

Students often find books through three methods: borrowing in libraries, buying in bookstores or purchasing online. However, books in libraries are often in short supply, those in bookstores might be expensive, and it takes time for shipping if purchased online. What if students want to get books instantly and at low cost? Ai the same time, there are some students who want to get rid of unneeded books. So how could those students connect with each other?

Solution Overview

I am providing students access to inexpensive books while providing others an outlet for their unneeded books. I designed an application which allows both groups to share the information of books they have/want, prices they feel comfortable with and a good location to exchange the books. This application allows them to reach the nearest users and connect with them.
This application works best when there are a lot of users, so I decided to firstly narrow down the community to just students on campus, where a large amount of students live. To avoid the problem of users not finding books and never coming back to use this APP again, I give users alternative option to post their needs first. Additionally, in order to attract more users and make this platform interesting, I will add one feature that users can pay with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.

Reflection.


According to the reading the Design of Everyday Things by Donald A.Norman, well-designed products should be clearly used with lessinstructions or even without any text-based information. Just thevisual, acoustic information themselves, being consistent with as many as possible of the four constraints, are enough to guide users to utilize them properly. I can’t agree more on Donald’s standpoint, so consider it as my most important design philosophy.
However, it is hard to achieve. After I built my paper prototypes(Figure 2 & 3) and showed them to my peers, they felt really confusedabout each feature, even though I explained all the time. Good design should be introductions the products themselves, but I explained a lot because they were way too much complex.


Figure 2. Too much content in one page and a lot of text near each button


Figure 3. Crowed layout makes users hard to understand

Changes.


It’s easy to get lost when designing the features of application, adding tedious details and redundant function buttons or pages, which I think would be necessary for users. But, that’s not true, because that’s the application design for designer myself, not users.
Only this one feature- share books with peers- already keeps this application complex, therefore, I decide to abandon the idea of “Second-hand Books Library” and keep the feature of “compare with other resources” as one simple button. I made this decision is not only because it follows my design philosophy, but also because a lot of target users and peers think it’s unnecessary.
After showing my paper prototypes to my peers and target users, I got a lot of feedbacks from them and added those buttons or indicators which I think proper right after they recommended. For example, I added slider on homepage (Figure 4) and I changed the drawer-shape menu to hamburger menu (Figure 5), which takes less space and wouldn’t cause confusion.

One main change that I made, which was suggested by several users, was I added one page to show all the user who have/want this book when clicking the book image on homepage, instead of leading to individual book page which posted by one user each time. Because of this change, I simultaneously moved some buttons (Figure 4.location button and user profile button) from homepage to this page (Figure 5 & 6), making homepage much more clean and simple.
Another main change that I made was I added “Want or Have” (Figure 5 & 6) option for users. It is hard to let this application work if there are only few users, because only if there are enough users, there would be enough books. Imagine if you wanted to find one book on this App, but found there was no one posted any information about this, you would feel frustrated. But if you have tried several times, things are still the same, you would feel disappointed and never turn back. In order to avoid this happening, I provide users the option of choosing “Want” as your purpose, which means even if there is no one “Have” this book right now, users can post needs first and let others to search their needs.
