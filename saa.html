File Management Consulting for Student Academic Affairs (SAA)

In this project, our team came up recommendations for Student Academic Affairs (SAA) in the University of Michigan College of Literature, Science and Arts (LSA) to alleviate the file management problem. We broke down the problem into 3 parts: technology (how to use Google Drive & M+ Box), naming protocol and implementation. My role was diving into the implementation section, finding out reasons why staff in SAA not following naming protocel and proposed recommendations.
See SAA website.
Client
UMichigan LSA Student Academic Affairs Office.
Skill

Problem Description

LSA Student Academic Affairs has multiple locations and mediums for storage of information (email, attachments, Google Drive, M+Box, internal websites, external websites), which makes it difficult to find information, as well as ensure information is consistent across all mediums. LSA Student Academic Affairs would like help in developing a process/guideline for how to store information. The current process is not being adhered to by the employees due to ease of use, lack of accountability, and lack of a logical process flow.

Key Findings

Key Finding 1:

Staff feel protocols don’t reflect their needs and perspective.


  •   •  “A good idea I’ll never use”
  •   •  A perception that top-down protocols are ineffective or irrelevant
  •   •  Lack of voice in design process
  •   •  Staff use ad hoc file management strategies they find personally useful, and that are not driven by the 2014 protocol

Key Finding 2:

Users lack understanding and training


  •   •  Staff relied heavily on Google Drive for their everyday workflows, but most struggled to understand its features and behaviors.
  •   •  M+Box is a distant-feeling system that staff feel uncomfortable using
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Finding 3:

Organizational change resistance


  •   •  Continued “shadow use” of Shared Drive by some users
  •   •  Cultural change resistance and reliance on old systems
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Recommendations

A User-Centered Redesign


This user-centered redesign can be broken up into three categories: Structural/Design Recomendations; Implementational Recomendations and Organizational Recomendations.


Design Recomendations.


  •   •  Use of a single Google Drive Structure.
Google drive can be set up for an entire organization to operate with one structure. This will concretize the structure and consistency of folder names and locations; avoid confusion over multiple individuals filing systems; allow for administration from a single point or individual and inherently standardize structure and look.


  •   •  Streamlined File Naming Convention
Simplify the file naming convention: reduce from 5 components to 2 components. Simpler is better even if it conveys less information due to the fact that it will be used period. Complexity leads to no use, inconsistency to no value.


Implementational Recomendations.


  •   •  Engage Users in Redesign Process.
Engage all employees throughout the design and implementation processes. Do user research and needs assessment and show this process as a benefit, not a burden. When people feel their needs have been considered and they feel that they are part of a change effort that change is much more likely to be permanent.


  •   •  Change Implementation Strategy.
  •   ·  Unfreeze: Communicate change process, necessity of change
  •   ·  Change: Implement change, engage users, abate change resistence and garner feedback.
  •   ·  Refreeze: Reward change champions and monitor outcomes.


Kurt Lewin’s 3 Stage Change Model

Organizational Recomendations.


  •   •  System Administrator(s).
We recognize that it may not be possible to hire on someone for this job. However we do believe that these responsbilities assigned to a single or more would significantly assist with non-adherence: Manage Permissions, Monitor Adherence, Design the file structure, Continuous Improvement.


  •   •  Training and Superusers.
Post design we suggest the following training elements:
  •   ·  Provide initial training session to educate team on Google Drive best practices, permissions, functionality.
  •   ·  Secondary training on organizational Google Drive structure.
  •   ·  Create or reinstate super-users at the team level.
  •   •  One Page Printed Reference Guide.
  •   ·  Include Administrator and Team Expert Contacts.
  •   ·  Visualization of single Drive Structure.
  •   ·  Quick Reference with example of File Naming Convention.


Conclusion

LSA SAA is a student centered organization dedicated to the development and growth of undergraduate students at University of Michigan. This function will always come first for the LSA SAA, therefore processes in place to support student interaction must use the least complex solutions to work well for all staff members. Existing file management procedures have faltered due to the complexity of protocols and the manner in which the last change iteration was rolled out. The existing design iteration is not supported by users in the office because they feel their needs have heard; as a result, staff feel estranged by the change process as opposed to an integral part of it. To create a new file management system that addresses these problems we recommend LSA SAA focus on user research in the next design iteration and implement our streamlined design recommendations centered on reducing complexity and redundancies within file management protocols. By utilizing these suggestions LSA SAA can better integrate file management into the workflow of users, garner ground-up support for change, and create processes that connect the potential value of the system to everyday use.
